Biography

I am a PhD student in Personality Psychology with a specialization in Statistics at the University of Minnesota. My research interests cover Personality, Identity, and Measurement. I mainly focus on longitudinal analyses of within-person change and stability, including both lifespan and intensive longitudinal data. Beyond substantive research interests, I am passionate about open science reforms and careful measurement considerations. I enjoy coding, data management, and data analysis.

Research

I view personality broadly as any psychological individual differences that may vary both between and within individuals. Beyond the Big Five taxonomy, I am interested in how traits interact at an individual level to form meaningful profile patterns. Further, I am fascinated by intraindividual variation across time and situation, both as a factor of lifespan development, meaningful situations, and informant perceptions.

Research Assistants

I am actively looking for undergraduate research assistants to start Fall 2021. To express your interest for this or future semesters, please email me at nguy4006@umn.edu with a complete application form and attach your CV and unofficial transcript. Mentorship statement

Requirements

  • A strong interest in statistics and methodology
  • No prior experience is required – I encourage early involvement and long-term collaboration
  • To create a mutually beneficial experience, you should be broadly interested in at least one of my substantive research programs – Personality, Identity, Measurement as outlined here [link to Research Interest session]

What to expect

  • Weekly team meeting: discuss journal articles, ongoing research projects, and potential research ideas
  • Reading: one journal article per week, with topics covering my substantive interests; meta-science and scientific reforms; methods and statistics. RAs are encouraged to propose and lead discussion sessions
  • Research tasks as needed: data cleaning and organizing, running participants, literature search and review, manuscript and conference presentation preparation

Some ongoing projects

  • The co-development of personality between adoptive and non-adoptive siblings
  • A deep dive into the Multigroup Ethnic Identity Measures: Psychometric properties and factor structures across large ethnically diverse samples
  • Identity processes and well-being: Exploring different types of identity exploration
  • Personality state distribution in relation to affects and relationship quality: A meta-analysis of intensive longitudinal data
