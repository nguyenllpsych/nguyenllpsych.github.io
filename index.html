Biography

I am a Personality Psychology PhD student with a specialization in Statistics at the University of Minnesota. My research interests cover Personality, Identity, and Measurement. I mainly focus on longitudinal trajectories of within-person change and stability, including both lifespan and intensive longitudinal data. Beyond substantive research interests, I am passionate about open science reforms and careful measurement considerations. I enjoy coding, data management, and data analysis.

Interests

  • Personality change and intraindividual variability
  • Identity exploration and narratives
  • Measurement and latent trait theory
